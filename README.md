# Hardware Trojan Detection and Localization in Verilog Designs
 **IEEE Research Publication**

This repository highlights the research work published in IEEE on **Hardware Trojan Detection and Localization in Verilog Designs for Renewable Systems**.

The work focuses on identifying malicious modifications (hardware trojans) in digital hardware designs and proposing techniques to detect and localize them within Verilog-based systems.

##  Research Overview

Hardware trojans are malicious modifications inserted into integrated circuits during the design or fabrication phase. These trojans can compromise the security, functionality, and reliability of hardware systems.

This research focuses on:

- Detection of hardware trojans in Verilog-based designs
- Localization of malicious logic within digital circuits
- Improving security for hardware used in renewable energy systems
- Strengthening trust in hardware supply chains

---

##  Methodology

The research methodology includes:

1. Designing digital hardware modules using Verilog
2. Introducing hardware trojan logic in controlled scenarios
3. Analyzing behavioral and structural changes in the circuit
4. Applying detection techniques to identify malicious modifications
5. Localizing trojan components within the design
