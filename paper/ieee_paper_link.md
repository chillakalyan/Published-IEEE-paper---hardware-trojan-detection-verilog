## Abstract (Summary)

Hardware trojans are malicious modifications inserted into integrated circuits that can compromise the security, reliability, and functionality of hardware systems. Detecting such threats is crucial, especially in critical infrastructure such as renewable energy systems.

This research proposes a methodology for detecting and localizing hardware trojans in Verilog-based digital designs. The approach analyzes structural and behavioral properties of the hardware design to identify suspicious logic and determine the exact location of malicious circuitry.

The proposed techniques improve trust in hardware design workflows and contribute to strengthening hardware security for renewable energy systems.
